Basics of Investing

Types of assets & ways of investing

  • shares
    • ETFs (exchange-traded funds)
    • shares of individual companies
    • commodity futures contracts
  • bonds
    • treasury bonds: inflation-indexed or with fixed rate
    • corporate bonds
  • real estate
    • apartments for rent
    • flipping houses
    • investment ground
  • raw materials
    • gold, silver
    • crude oil, natural gas
  • foreign currencies
  • cryptocurrencies
  • ...and many more others

Types of portfolios & recommended division

  • financial cushion
    • basic requirement for a peaceful and secure life, so that you are no longer a slave to your employer and the financial system
    • should be enough to cover your expenses for about 3 to 12 months (depending on your risk tolerance)
    • should be invested in safe assets
    • about 25% should be kept as cash or in a bank savings or deposit account
    • about 75% should be held in government bonds, which are relatively very safe
    • encourages you to follow your ideas in a career, e.g. you no longer have to worry about losing your job, because you have enough money to live on for next 6 months
  • long-term (defensive) portfolio
    • main portfolio, which should be invested in diversified assets
    • its main purpose is to grow your money over time in a stable manner, without need to spend many hours on it
    • a good target is 3% above inflation
    • you should define wages (and acceptable ranges) for every asset in your portfolio to automate the investment process and avoid being fooled by your feelings or environment
    • there are many assets you can invest in, but a popular one is to invest 50% in financial instruments and 50% in real estate
    • don't worry about the 100% exact division of your portfolio, you don't have to buy properties right away, you can start with financial instruments and then buy properties later
    • recommended division of the financial instruments part is:
    • 40% in inflation-indexed treasury bonds
    • 30% in shares (both developed and developing markets)
    • 20% in treasury bonds listed on the market
    • 10% in investment gold
  • offensive portfolio
    • additional portfolio, which consists of money we can afford to lose
    • consists of assets of higher risk, but also higher potential return
    • good option if you want to spend more time on investing and utilize your knowledge in harsh market conditions
    • here you can be more flexible with wages and try to predict the prices of assets, it's hard, but possible

Set of basic rules

  • the economy and markets are cyclical
  • don't put all your eggs into one basket - every few or a dozen or so years there is a time that is catastrophic for one of the asset classes, so diversify your portfolio
  • buy low, sell high
  • be greedy when others are fearful, be fearful when others are greedy; buy when blood is spilling
  • it is not profitable to hold large sums of cash in the long term, most assets bring higher rates of return
  • controlling emotions and mental state is often more important than knowledge
  • no one knows the future, don't trust back analysis
  • everyone has a different tendency to take risks
